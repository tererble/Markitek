How to Extend Your House in Malaysia (2026 Complete Guide)

Written by Markitek • Updated February 2026 • 8 min read

Planning to extend your house in Malaysia? Before starting construction, you must understand local authority (PBT) approval requirements to avoid fines or demolition orders.

This guide explains the approval process, required drawings, consultant involvement, and common mistakes homeowners make.

1. Do You Need Approval for House Extension?

In Malaysia, most house extensions require approval from your local council (PBT). This includes porch extension, kitchen extension, additional rooms, and roof changes.

Construction without approval can result in fines or stop-work orders.

2. What Drawings Are Required?

  • Existing floor plan
  • Proposed floor plan
  • Roof plan
  • Elevation drawings
  • Structural drawings (if required)

Some councils also require endorsement by a registered architect or engineer.

3. How Long Does Approval Take?

Approval typically takes between 1–3 months depending on documentation completeness and council workload.
